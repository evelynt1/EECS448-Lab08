Cheetahs

Cheetahs are known to be the fastest land animal. They are said to reach speeds of 50-80 mph, and usually weigh anywhere between 46-160 lbs as an adult. The scientific name for a cheetah is Acinonyx jubatus. They are known for the teardrop next to their eyes. Unlike most cats, they hunt during the day and actually don't roar/aren't loud. Their coat provides a means of camouflage and make them successful hunters as they are not very easily spotted. Fun fact: An adult cheetah can have anywhere between 2000 to 3000 spots on his/her body!
